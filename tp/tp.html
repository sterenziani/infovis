A Study on my Gaming Habits

I've kept track of what games I've played starting October 2012 to analyze how my consumption of videogames has evolved over the years.

Some of the stats taken into account include hours spent on each game, dates I picked up and dropped each game and whether I finished each game or not (or if it doesn't apply, as is usually the case for multiplayer-focused games or sandbox simulators)

What types of games do I like?

Obervations:
  • I play a lot of Japanese Role-Playing games, which are known for their length, so it should come as no surprise that the genre makes up a fair share of my playtime.

  • I'm not particularly into shooter games, and yet the two games I did get into make up a decent amount of my playtime, although a decent amount of those hours was probably spent waiting for an online match to start or looking for a server to connect to.

What series do I like?

Obervations:
  • Once again, Team Fortress 2 makes my love for its genre and series seem bigger than it is, taking the spotlight despite that being the only enntry in its series that I played.

  • The Legend of Zelda and Fire Emblem series are more deserving of their bubbles, balancing the many hours logged into them across several games.

  • Despite the limited number of games in the Bravely Default and Xenoblade series, I've logged plenty of hours into them, since they are long RPGs.

Which genres take me the longest to finish?

Obervations:
  • The RPGs on the shorter side tend to be either short extensions to a longer RPG, or made by western developers.

  • I haven't played enough campaigns of simulator games to make a good estimate of how long they may be, but the ones I've played are very close in length.

  • Action, rhythm and horror games tend to be on the shorter side.

When did I play games the most?

Obervations:
  • I stopped playing as much once I started college in 2016

  • From that point onward, I played almost exclusively during summer and winter breaks (January, February, July and December)

How long do games stay in my backlog?

I only tend to buy physical games when abroad, and I buy digital games when they're on sale. So it's common to buy a lot of games on the same week just to leave those games sitting on the shelf until I decide to play them.

Obervations:
  • I don't actually take that long to try out a game once I buy it, seeing as most games follow a straight line

  • I generally play a game within three months of buying it, with exceptions.
